i wish i have a friend like you...

welcome to our forum, i hope you learn from us and may join us at one of or tastings

the gold is 40% abv, it is light for some, but can be high for some

as you say, you do not drink whiskey, i would recommend to take just a little sip, and hold it on your tongue for 10 seconds to aloud some mouth water to reduce the strength, than swallow
you may find that way lighter and enjoyable, as more flavors will come through

if that was still hard for your palate, just add a tiny drop of water and than try it again

if it is still hard add another tiny bit, but do not add too much at a one time, because that can "kill" the whiskey

once you find the right amount you need to add (got the right abv to you) than you will love whiskey

enjoy your journey, and please share your experience

As regards to water that really is down to you. It is never any harm to add a few drops to open the whiskey. by Good Whiskey Hunting » Sun Jan 05, 2014 5:55 am

I always have a little water with me to test with a whiskey. I had an Ardbeg 10 year old earlier and it amazes me how much different it tastes with a few drops of water add to it.

Jameson Gold is great with or without I think. With the stronger whiskeys its always good idea to have a glass of water anyway, not only to add to it but to re hydrate. I've learnt the hard way on many the cask strength bottle.
