I sold a lot of Kilbeggan Hot Whiskeys over the winter in the bar. I rimmed the glass with nutmeg, studded lemon with cloves, brown sugar and a cinnamon stick as a stirrer. Was a big hit, I think Kilbeggan handles dilution, be it hot or cold, very well. Infact when someone asks me for a whiskey I just give them Kilbeggan, we sell more of that than Jameson.

I'd always use powers, paddy or jameson for a hot whiskey, mainly powers, and like IWC etc. would use a clove studded lemon and sugar. I never felt the malt based blends (bushmills kilbeggan etc.) really worked. Maybe that just that they don't match the flavour I expect from a hot whiskey over the years which is more a relfection on the hot whiskeys I would have been reared on. (Not literally).

I think the best hot whiskey I've had was a Paddy in my uncles old farmhouse in front of a big log fire after a long walk in the Nire valley / Comeragh area. Sometimes the context is everything.
