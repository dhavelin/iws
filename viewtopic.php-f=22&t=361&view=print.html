Hi Shane,

Despite the fact that the New World wine industry is moving gradually towards screw-tops from corking, I have to say that in general (for both wine and whiskey) I much prefer those with a cork. Can't say why exactly; perhaps it's just an appearance thing! On the bottle-colour issue, I personally feel that a clear bottle is better becasue it allows you to examine the contents more efficiently.

Shape is very important, as is colour. People tend to be attracted to the darker whiskies, so if it's naturally dark, it's a good idea to have it in a clear bottle.

And bar staff will tell you that (I think they will anyway) when customers see unusually striking bottles like the Kilbeggan 15, they go for it more than if it was in a normal bottle.

Then there's Michael Collins, which doesn't fit on bar shelves...
