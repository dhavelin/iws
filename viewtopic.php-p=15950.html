Society Scotch Whisky Bottling - A Hair of an Idea...

by Luke Gough » Thu Jan 10, 2013 11:50 am

Ladies and Gentlemen,

Should we, The Society, examine the possibility of selecting and acquiring our own bottling of a Scotch Whisky?

My reason for suggesting this now is that existing stocks of Scotch Whisky (for sane money) are running low due to worldwide demand (Great Recession how are ye! )

If so, is there a preferred region/style/distillery and at what price per bottle? (Incl. VAT and Duty paid )

Thoughts and comments please!

Re: Society Scotch Whisky Bottling - A Hair of an Idea...

by DavidH » Thu Jan 10, 2013 2:21 pm

Worth kicking around the idea, certainly.

For me, it depends what we would be forgoing on the Irish side. The society can contribute to the diversity and perception of Irish whiskey by releasing its own bottlings. If we don't do it, there is no guarantee that someone else will. A Scotch would be interesting to members but wouldn't have the same impact outside the society.

I suppose I'd like to see the Irish options laid out, alongside our budget. Comparing with some well-chosen Scotch options would be useful, I'm sure.
