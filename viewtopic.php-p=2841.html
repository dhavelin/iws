What's the best Irish whiskey?

by jcskinner » Wed May 06, 2009 4:16 pm

What's your 'desert island' Irish whiskey?
If you could only have one bottle, what would it be?
No qualifying criteria other than the dram must qualify as whiskey (ie distilled from grains, at least 40 proof, aged in wood no less than three years) and Irish (distilled in Ireland).
No cheating by rattling off a list of your 'best grain', 'best potstill', 'best blend' etc.
I just want to know what is the ONE Irish whiskey you rate above all others and, most importantly, why.
Do you rate Black Bush or Paddy for their value? Knappogue 51 or Dungourney for their rarity? Some other whiskey for some other reason?
I'm keen to know what are the best Irish whiskeys according to the IWS forum posters. You guys drink Irish whiskey - tell me what's your top dram and why!

I'll kick off - the one Irish whiskey I rate above all others is (currently!) the Bushmills 21 year old. I think its port element and complexity narrowly edges the 25 yo millennium single casks, which are sometimes a teeny bit woody, and since I really love their base spirit above all others, this for me is the apogee of what Irish whiskey can do. Maybe you agree?

Re: What's the best Irish whiskey?

by DavidH » Wed May 06, 2009 4:45 pm

Tyrconnell 15yo single cask.

The smell of it is so cheerful and inviting. Bags of taste too. The wood is there but only enough to drag your attention back to the whiskey so you are conscious of what you are drinking. Lovely warm finish, no dryness creeping in.

I also like the fact I can go back to the shop and get another bottle so I don't have to be too precious about doling it out.

That said, I haven't tried the Bushies you mention. I suspect I'd quite like the port-influenced single malt.

I could see myself with a bottle of Ally's Midleton Single Cask too, but only on the basis of a small tasting, so that would need further research. JohnM says the Jameson RVR is even better so maybe that...

Since I can have any bottle I want, I'm also tempted to request something rare and expensive just to try it. Like the Knappogue 1951.

But I'll be perfectly happy with my Tyrconnell 15yo.

That is a very hard question to answer as I find I drink different different whiskies to suit different moods or whims.

Some of the super whiskies are distant memories for me as many of them have only been samples so I will only base my selections on full bottles that I have opened myself.

The Bushmills 21 is indeed a super whiskey but a Bushmills sherry cask is hard to beat for me. I'm also very taken by the Cadenheads Peated whiskies and I think the 13yo has been my favourite so far and would love it to be a regular bottling but the one I keep going back to (as I currently can) is the Redbreast 15yo which I rate slightly above the Jameson 15yo.

However I may open my Midleton 25yo PPS in the not so distant future and change my mind as it is looking more and more like a bargain the more I think about it
