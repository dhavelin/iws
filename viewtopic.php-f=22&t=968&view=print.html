Cooley - Best distillery in EU (again)

Posted: Thu Nov 18, 2010 2:01 pm
by Malt-Teaser
I just received this press release:

"Cooley Still Champions of Europe

Cooley Distillery named European Distiller of the Year for 3rd Consecutive Year

Cooley Distillery, Ireland’s only independent whiskey distiller, has been named European Distiller of the Year for the third year running at the 2010 International Wine and Spirit Competition Awards banquet in London last night.
This latest accolade follows Cooley’s 10 gold medals haul from the IWSC in July of this year which brought its total to 100 gold medals over the last 10 years. This follows on for Cooley’s earlier success of being named 2010 Distillery of the Year by Malt Advocate, the leading whisky magazine in North America.

To date Cooley has won the European Distiller of the Year title three times (2008, 2009, 2010), been named World Distiller of the Year by the IWSC (2008) and in 2009 Cooley’s Kilbeggan 15-year-old blended whiskey collected the prestigious Brown Forman Trophy for Worldwide Whiskey of the Year.

Jack Teeling, Managing Director, commented, “To be the best in Europe three years in a row is a fantastic achievement for everyone involved in Cooley Distillery and is an Irish success story we can all be proud of in these challenging times. Our success is based on innovation, a range of world class whiskeys and lot of hard work.”

The awards were accepted on the night by Noel Sweeney, Master Blender and Jack Teeling, Managing Director of Cooley Distillery, at the 41st IWSC Awards ceremony at the Guildhall in London."

Well done.
Malty
