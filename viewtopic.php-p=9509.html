The L code for 2010 could be both 10 or 0. It possibly depends on when and possibly where it was bottled.

I may be a scenario that they went with 0 for a while and decided to go with 2 digits there after.

Before it was always only 1 digit with o standing for 2000 1 for 2001 2 for 2002 etc. But you also have some of these codes pre-dating that also so you could have say 8 which could stand for 1998 and 2008 . So possibly the 2 digit L code has been brought in to rectify this.
