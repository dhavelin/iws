Posted: Sat Feb 21, 2015 11:10 pm
by John
Here are some of my tasting notes on this new release.

Nose: Rich, sweet, dark fruit; certainly more spicy than you would expect.

Palate: Smooth, sweet, oily & spicy (All good so far as SPS goes!) The mouthfeel is excellent, the 'Christmas Cake' complexity is there, though it does feel that the cake has certainly taken on a new dimension.

Finish: Long, lingering, excellent; the sherry maturation gradually loses influence to the central character of the familiar Redbreast SPS.

Impression: I really like it! This is relatively new territory for sure and from conversations I've had over the past two weeks, it has divided opinions. Those opinions continue to be offered though - which is great! It means that members are still exploring and analysing and spending the time appreciating it; rather than just giving an off-the-cuff assessment. I hope this is the first of many sherry-matured expressions; including perhaps PX even(?!). However, as a member of the IWS and having had the opportunity to taste some of the cask-strength sherry-matured SPS, I would love to see Midleton release such a version!
