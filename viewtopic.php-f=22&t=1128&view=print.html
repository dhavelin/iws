Hi Fion치n, true Powers SPS may be a second cousin from the Redbreast but it is once removed

To be honest they are very different whiskeys. You are obviously aware of the taste profile of the Redbreast 12yo at this stage. The Redbreast gets a significant amount of it's character from the Sherry cask content. The make up of the Redbreast 12yo is 70% bourbon cask and 30% Sherry cask and that 30% adds a whole pile of sherry charachter. However the Powers John's Lane SPS is over 90% bourbon barrelwith a small amount of sherry cask which is in single ration figures. Even more interesting it uses a much heavier pot still style which gives it a much more robust style with the spice and wood being quite prominent. ANd it produces a wonderfully beefier whiskey in comparison to the others and even the Regular Powers. As Heidi Donnelan mentioned it was very much a masculine whiskey.
