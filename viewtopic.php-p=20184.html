Some of what you say is true but happened regularly. If a year was not selling well they would hold off the release of the newer year by a month or so.

However they basically re-alligned the release schedule. Up to 2009 the Midleton was released mainly in time for the Christmas market (circa October I believe) which is when majority of premium whiskey is sold in Ireland and around the world, up to 90% of Premium Irish whiskey sales happen on the run up to Christmas I believe.

However in the advent of premium whiskey becoming more accessed by the masses it was seen as not ideal having a Annual release with the year on it being released near the end of the calender year. Therefore 2009 was released as per previous years near the end of the year but was produced as a smaller batch as 2010 was then released early in the following year (2010 obviously) as a fairly normal batch size but it is possible that the 2010 is a slightly smaller batch from normal too but to be honest that is just a guess from me.
