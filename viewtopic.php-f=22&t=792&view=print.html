James McAllister and Sons are the likely source of your bottle.
http://www.potstill.de/oldads.htm includes a pic of one of their ads.
They were apparently operational from at least the 1850s, but when Alfred Barnard visited Ireland in the late 1880s he made no mention of them having a functioning distillery, though they appear to have continued marketing whiskey well after that date.
My best guess is that they were bottlers and bonders. There were plenty such in Ireland at that time, bottling own label whiskeys, and Mitchells are the last of that honourable tradition in Ireland today.
I know Irish Whiskey Chaser possesses a bottle of one brand of theirs called Clan Colla, so perhaps he might be able to shed more light?
