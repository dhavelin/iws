Potato-peel is another name for potato skin.

However the reference is to what was once a state run distillery near the town of Dundalk which made industrial spirit. Possibly created in response to the great fuel shortages in the 70's. For many years it was mixed with Petrol that was sold in Ireland as part of a self sustaining industry and as a way to reduce our national imported fuel bill.

This plant was closed well before 1989 and Cooley took over and converted it to a proper whiskey distillery in 1989.

The Riverstown plant, now Cooley Distillery, was part of Ceimicí Teo ("Chemicals Limited") which was established in 1939. There were four plants in total I think. The idea was to make industrial alcohol from potatoes.
