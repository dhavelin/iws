What whiskeys will 2015 bring??

by Good Whiskey Hunting » Mon Jan 05, 2015 1:24 am

This will be an interesting year for Irish whiskey. For the first time in generations we could have Whiskeys from more than the big 3. Both Alltech and Dingle have stock that could be legally sold this year. Both were distilling in November 2012.

Midleton didn't released the promised 3 SPS's so I'm assuming they will make amends this year. We should have the Red Spot very soon.
I'm assuming a Jameson Pot Still will be introduced.
I expect a couple of single cask Midleton's.

I have been hoping for a new brand, a Dungourney perhaps to add to the Dungourney 1964 legacy. I'd like to see some old labels reintroduced like Hewitts.

Beam Suntory could have a few treats for us this year. They have rebranded the Greenore as Kilbeggan Single Grain. They too have Sinsle Pot Still and could have some limited releases just to test the water.

The Kilbeggan 21 went down well at WhiskyLive. They may decide to give the malts a spot in the sunlight this year.

I'm really look forward to see what Bushmills will do now that they have been released from the shackles of Diageo. I'm may be too soon for them to do anything innovative but I've great expectations.

I can only wait to see what Teelings have in store for us, one things for certain it won't be dull. They don't do that.

Re: What whiskeys will 2015 bring??

by Good Whiskey Hunting » Sat Dec 26, 2015 11:57 am

Teelings came out with great whiskeys this year. I've yet to try them all. I'm sure they'll have a few for next year too.
They have released Kavanagh's in the US and I've heard good things about it.

IDL come up with Dair Ghaelach and Powers three Swallow. These will be new expressions going forward.

We also got a one of Redbreast, the Mano A Lámh. It sold for €65+ p&p. I was very impressed with it particularly because it was released through their singlepotstill.com site for members. This has to be a first for them.

One big change in policy from them is the new single cask Powers. We've got the Aviation & Celtic Whiskey Shop releases so far.

Kilbeggan are keeping things tight with no new releases but I'm sure there stock cooking. They had us busy with the Kilbeggan 21 & Connemara 22 in 2014.

We had Bushmills at Whiskylive in Dublin this year. I was a the society stand just across from them. They had lots of visitors. Seamus Lowry, the brand ambassador and Colum Egan, master distiller were on the stand. I got some great sample to try and things look good for the future. Diageo is gone now & will not be missed. Plans are in place to expand production. I'm looking forward to seeing what new expressions will follow.

Dingle produces a whiskey as predicted. It was a limited edition Cask 2. I'm told it's nice but I've yet to try it.
They are producing Malt as well as Pot Still now. They're a great little distillery. I hope other that have started follow in their footsteps.

Altech didn't come out with a new whiskey. They have stopped production in Carlow for the time being. I know they have be able to move to Dublin yet so things are a little stagnant for them. Fingers crossed that they'll get back in action soon.

West Cork Distillers have a brand out but it's not sold in Ireland. They have introduced The Pogues Irish Whiskey which is available in Supervalu for €30. Frank McHardy is behind the production so it should e an improvement on previous efforts.

The Irish Whiskey Society came out with our first ever Grain. This was sourced from Midleton and was also their first ever release of a grain.
