by Distiller » Tue Jun 10, 2014 8:27 am

Not a lot in the press so I thought I would post the latest reviews from the BTI in Chicago. They did a rather large review of Irish Whiskey at the end of May.

You can see the complete list at http://www.tastings.com/ (point out of 100, whiskey name, price, tasting notes, date)

98 • Teeling 21 Year Old Single Malt Vintage Reserve Irish Whiskey $199.99/700 ml.
Brushed amber copper color with an olive cast. Layered aromas and flavors of dark raisin toast, honey butter, lightly smoked peat, dried peaches, and leather with a dry-yet-fruity medium-full body and a complex roasted whole nut, dried flower, honeycomb, and delicately smoky mineral accented finish with noticeable wood tannins. A very complex, long-aged Irish whisky for contemplative sipping and cigars. (tasted on May-29-2014)

97 • Jameson Rarest Vintage Reserve Irish Whiskey $207/700ml.
Pale amber color. Complex aromas and flavors of caramelized roasted pecans, honey pastry, dried fruit, suede, brown spices with a supple, dry-yet-fruity and a very long and evolving, complex raisin chutney, passionfruit, creamy vanilla, and pepper accented finish. A superb marriage of grain and oak. (tasted on Jun-01-2014)

96 • Midleton Barry Crockett Legacy Irish Whiskey $139/700ml.
Golden amber color. Aromas of candied dried apricot, granola, and white blossoms with a silky, fruity-yet-dry medium-to-full body and a round, creme brulee, brown spice, coconut macaroon, and chalk accented finish. A wonderfully balanced and elegant Irish whiskey. (tasted on May-29-2014)

96 • Redbreast 12 Year Old Cask Strength Irish Whiskey $72.85/700ml.
Golden amber color. Vibrant aromas of honeyed granola and orange gelato with a rich, fruity-yet-dry full body and a long, scintillating finish with a cascade of baking spices, toffee, and minerals. Rounds out beautifully with a little spring water. A superb Irish whisky of the highest order. (tasted on May-29-2014)

95 • Bushmills 16 Year Old Single Malt Irish Whiskey $65.
Rich amber color. Aromas of salty caramelized roasted nuts, custard, dried tropical fruits, and cocoa with a silky dryish medium-to-full body and a rich melange of dried fruits and berries, dusty grain, gravel, honeycomb, and peppery spices. Impressively rich and satisfying. (tasted on May-31-2011)

95 • Redbreast 21 Year Old Irish Whiskey $150/700ml.
Golden amber color. Exotic aromas of passionfruit, guava, nettles, chocolate covered pineapple and honey buttered scones with a silky, fruity-yet-dry medium-to-full body and long, resonant ground grain, nougat, dried flower, and green tobacco leaf finish. A complex after dinner Irish whisky and a perfect foil for a milder cigar. (tasted on May-29-2014)

94 • Bushmills Rare 21 Year Old Single Malt Irish Whiskey $120.
Brilliant amber color. Aromas of banana creme brulee, flower patch, honeyed fig cake, and African spices with a silky, dry-yet-fruity medium-to-full body and a creme Anglaise, orange Pekoe tea, mossy stone, dusty grain, and oak spice finish. A nicely framed oak aged Irish whisky. (tasted on May-31-2011)

94 • Knappogue Castle 14 Year Old Single Malt Irish Whiskey $60.
Old gold color. Rich aromas of honeyed raisins, blossoms, and buttery fruit custard pastry with a silky, fruity-yet-dry medium-to-full body and a long, grainy, nut, pepper and dried fruit driven finish. A lush, flavorful Irish whiskey that will shine with cigars. (tasted on Jun-01-2014)
