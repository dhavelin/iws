Reading Lot / Date Codes

by WhiskeyDad » Tue Nov 18, 2014 11:41 pm

Hello. I was wondering if there are any standard bottling codes on Irish whiskey bottles. I don't have any rare or old bottles, so it's more of a curiosity. Sometimes I hear people rave about a certain year even if it's a blend and then say recent bottlings are not as good. Just wondering how to tell when something was bottled.

For example, I have a bottle of Redbreast 12 that I just purchased at a great price (compared to most places anyway). I love this stuff, but have heard a few people claim it's quality dropped off as it got more popular. Code on the bottle says: L316831148 14:24.

I assume the 14:24 might imply 2014 and week 24 or something? Anyone know?

Re: Reading Lot / Date Codes

by WhiskeyDad » Thu Nov 20, 2014 7:09 pm

Well the good folks at Irish Distillers were quick to return my e-mail. Here is what they said about this date code:

Hope this email finds you well. Regarding your code, please see below details:

· L = Lot Code

· 3 = Year 2013

· 168 = Julian date so 168 is June 17th

· 3 = Code to identify the Bottling Plant, 3 is our bottling plant located in Waterford.

· 1 = Bottling Line 1

· 148 = Rotation of Vat Number i.e. how many batches the bottling plant will have processed that year in that particular holding vessel (Vat). Not only Redbreast but other spirits will have been held in that Vat throughout the year.
