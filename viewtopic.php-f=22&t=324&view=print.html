by TheWhiskeyBro
Yes these guys bought a cask of Single Malt (aka Tyrconnell) from Cooley and chucked in a bottle of Preston's (Malt/PPS/Blend???). This process yielded 330 bottles which they marketed (cashed in) for the millennium. So technically it is a blend (329 parts malt to 1 part Preston's). So it was bottled in 1999 and since Cooley first produced malt around 1989, the malt is very likely to be less than 10 years old. In terms of value, it is more of a curiousity than anything else... difficult to put a value on it, but it would not have broad appeal other than to a few collectors.
