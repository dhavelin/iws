The Whiskey Collection - The Loop Dublin Airport

by TheWhiskeyBro » Wed Dec 29, 2010 5:19 pm

This thread is for updates on the availability of exclusive and hard to find Irish Whiskey in Terminal 2, Duty Free @ 'The Loop' in Dublin Airport.

Greenore 19 Single Grain Single Cask #1798 d. 05.12.90 b. 29.10.10 @ 60.5% 300 bottles €195
Midleton Bourbon PPS Single Cask #48709 d. 12.11.91 b. 10.10.10 @ 53.7% 204 bottles? €250
Bushmills Bourbon Barrel Single Cask #298? d. c.1993 b. c.2006? @ 56.5% €175 (Maison du Whisky)

Although the above prices are eye watering also worth noting in terms of availability are:

John L. Sullivan Irish Whiskey 1L @ 40.0%
Kellan Irish Whiskey 1L @ 40.0%
Jameson Distillers Selection Old Irish Whiskey Aged 12 Years d. 1993 b. 2005? @ 43.0% 5,000 bottles.

If you arrive early in Terminal 1 in Dublin Airport, once you are through security and airside you can walk to your left after security, walk to the end of the corridor turn right , take lifts down 1 level at you will find the Irish Whiskey Collection down to the right hand corner of the duty free shopping area after you come out of the lifts. (About 10/15 mins walk). You can use the shop and collect service if buying at non-duty free prices.

It appears they are continually trying to source whiskeys to ensure they have the largest duty free selection of Irish Whiskey.

Re: The Whiskey Collection - The Loop Dublin Airport

by jcskinner » Fri Jan 07, 2011 4:22 pm

Don't want to drag this thread off topic at all, but I've been through Heathrow's World of Whiskies twice recently and the prices really do put Dublin to shame. £30 for Bush 16 yo, or £15 for Tyrconnell, for example.
However, they simply don't have anything like a decent selection of Irish whiskey at all. (Even worse when it comes to Japanese or World whiskies.)
They told me there this morning that they'd love to stock more Irish whiskeys but simply can't source the stock! Can't get the Redbreast 15 yo, or older Tyrconnells or Connemaras, or even the Bush 21 yo. There's not even any Powers or Paddys.
Instead, they have a moribund little corner of standard Jamesons, Bush and some Tyrconnell, Kilbeggan blend and regular Connemara, dwarfed by a spectacular range of Scotch.
I think that the Irish distillers are missing an opportunity here. The WoW guys told me they get asked about Irish whiskeys all the time, but just can't source them from the manufacturers. Apparently they recently asked IDL about a limited edition, and instead were given the travel retail signature reserve.
Heathrow is a major hub for whiskey retail, and in the few minutes I spent there this morning, they must have sold in the region of a £1,000 worth of Scotch. Their prices are keen, and they have a lot of people coming through the place, including many people who are keen to purchase limited, rare and expensive whiskey.
I'm delighted to see that Dublin airport now stocks a great range of Irish whiskey. I hope the Irish whiskey industry will build on this and seek to emulate the Dublin range in other airports like Heathrow.
