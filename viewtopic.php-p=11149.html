Whiskey menu @ Lanigan's Bar on Eden Quay, Dublin.

by kurtbenoit » Fri Sep 16, 2011 11:41 pm

Hi everyone, just wanted to let you know that we at Lanigan's have a proper whiskey menu as of tonight. For anyone who doesn't know Lanigan's, it's number 11 on Eden Quay, just beside O'Connell bridge in Dublin's city centre.

It took longer than I expected (I'm only working there part time and the rest of the staff seemed uninterested) but it's done now and I hope you'll all call in.

It's not the most extensive menu and as I said I'm the only bartender in there who has an interest in it, so don't expect to learn anything new as you might in Mulligan's. But I focused on offering as fair a price as I could (for a city centre pub) and fully intend to have promotions most months. On that topic, our opening offers are as follows.

Redbreast 15 Year Old 7.50
Jameson 18 Year Old 8.50
Midleton Very Rare 2011 10.00

This opening offer will run until November 1st at the very earliest and possibly beyond. November's promotion will see the standard Cooley range, Kilbeggan, Tyrconnell, Connemara and Greenore available for just 4 euro a shot and that will run for the whole of November.

Good stuff Martin, i hope you said "I told you so!"

Good Whiskey Hunting, How many whiskeys does your bar have at present? you dont need lots to have a whiskey menu. My first menu started out with 10 whiskies on it that had been gathering dust in the bar, the owner asked me to "get rid of them" i.e. sell them out. So i printed a little A6 sheet with them priced up and shifted them in one weekend, needless to say the owner changed his tune. I started the the whisky menu in Brooks along the same lines and that grew and grew and grew! L Mulligan Grocer has been the same, well we started with 50ish and now have 200+.
Why not approach your employer and ask if you can put together a little price list of the whiskies( im assuming you have the Jameson and Bushmills ranges and probably a bottle or 2 off scotch?) Once theres a price list sales go up as people are often scared to ask the barman the price of a whisky or order it without the price, it a catch 22 for the customer. If the whisky is priced up the customer can then ordered a whisky that suits their budget.
Ive helped several bars in Ireland start out this way and would be willing to help you if you need it, i also have a rake of glencairn glasses that im willing to sell to any bar at cost price.
