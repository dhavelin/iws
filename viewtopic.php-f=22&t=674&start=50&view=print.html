Started watching "Copper", on Showcase here in Canada, on Sunday. It is a police drama set in the Five Points slums of New York City in 1864. The show focuses on an Irish American (I think the dialogue implied that he came over to the US as a young child) detective by the name of Corcoran who appears to be one of the law-abiding cops of that time period. It's focus is split between crime fighting and the ongoing bribery and other general skullduggery going on within elite society at that time, as it pertained to the "management and re-development" (i.e. how to make the most money) of the slums of New York at that time.

No, Daniel Day Lewis and Leo DiCaprio do not make a cameo in this show (they died the previous year in "Gangs of New York").

Whiskey is the primary drink of choice throughout the first two episodes, though with no brand specific references. I would also think that it would be a safer drink of choice than regular water, given the lack of sanitation in most of NYC during that time period.

McNulty: Jameson's, please.
Bartender: Bushmills alright?

Bartender(glibly): The price is right, ain't it?
