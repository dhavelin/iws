More details on the Awards.

Quoting my email from Ally.

"Hi,

Delighted to announce details of the Irish Whiskey Awards 2014 which are being held at Kilbeggan Distillery on Thursday 23rdOctober 2014.

My original intention was to rotate the awards around the Irish distilleries on a yearly basis and with the present rate of expansion we should be able to fill the diary for the next 25 years! Due to venue size this year, we have to limit the audience to 80 people (40 trade & 40 consumers). Ticket price will include coach from Dublin city centre to Kilbeggan and back, hot & cold canapes, Irish Whiskey cocktails on arrival, tour of Kilbeggan distillery & tasting of all this year’s winning whiskeys. Tickets are priced at €54.50 and will include a donation of €14.50 per ticket to Mary’s Meals http://www.marysmeals.ie/ which should raise enough money to feed 80 kids in the poorest parts of the world for 1 year – that’s 29200 dinners!

Event: Irish Whiskey Awards 2014
Date: Thursday 23rd October, 2014
Time: Coach leaves Dublin city centre at 4.15pm sharp, coach to depart Kilbeggan distillery at around 9.30pm.
If you want to make alternative travel plans the tours, canapes & cocktails will start at 5.30pm at Kilbeggan distillery with the awards ceremony starting at 7.30pm.
Venue: Kilbeggan Distillery, County Westmeath

Tickets: €54.50, only available from the Celtic Whiskey Shop or by phone 01 675 9744. The number is strictly limited to 80 and we can’t exceed that amount, no exceptions. Apologies but we aren’t taking reservations only ticket sales.

Sláinte

Ally"

Redbreast 21 won overall whiskey of the year.

it also won Single Pot still Whiskey

Single Malt 12 years and under went to Jack Ryan's 12 year old

Single Malt 13 and over went to Teelings 21 year old

Teelings also won Best Grain and Best Poitin

Best Blend under €60 went to Powers 12 year old

Best Blend over €60 went to Jameson Gold

Best Cask Strength went to Redbreast Cask strength

Best Single Cask went to Celtic Cask Naoi.
