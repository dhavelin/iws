Hi John,
quite seriously the Bushmills 16y is a very good whisky although it didn't quite win a gold medal.
It received a score of 85 points (as an average from all ten judges) which gave it a silver medal overall in MMA 2012.

In addition we have three whisky categories defined by price. Bushmills 16y fits into the Daily Dram category (up to â‚¬50) and within that category it won overall "Best Sherried Whisky", a separate award from the actual medals. We know that it isn't 100% sherry casks, but the Oloroso influence is predominant and as Oloroso casks are used, it qualified to be considered for the "Sherried" award. In fact after the A'Bunadh Batch 39 won the overall "Supreme Whisky" (in the DD category) the Bushmills 16y was the obvious "Sherried" winner.

So congratulations (from me) on Bushmills 16y winning the "BEst Sherried Whisky" in our Daily Drams category, also on it winning an individual silver medal.
Congratulations also to Bushmills 21y for winning an overall Bronze medal (84pts) and to Bushmills 10y for an overall Bronze medal (80pts).

In addition Redbreast 12y won a Bronze medal (83pts)
Yellow Spot 12y a Bronze medal (83pts)

From my point of view it was excellent to see Irish Whiskey so well represented this year and with such good quality too.
Congratulations again,
Keith
