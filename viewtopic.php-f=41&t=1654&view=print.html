Company was W.H.White & Co. Limerick ----long gone. Original premises was I believe
'the White house' pub @ corner of O'Connell & Glentworth Streets. Latterly I think may have
ended up in Thomas St. before they ceased to trade. Also bottled John Jameson 10 year old
using their own very distinctive label as 'White's Red Label' liqueur whiskey of which I have
an old bottle.

Basically per mid 1960's most whiskey in Ireland was bottled by the retailer. Mainly pubs etc so you may find many variations of labels. When you say red label is it a small kidney shaped label if so on the bulbous part of the neck. If a rectangle stating an age under the main label near the base.

I'm afraid I reckon that it is worthless ... and if it was to be worth something the label is in too bad of a condition.

I reckon the Number to be a retailer number issued by the Distillery. Each retailer would have a unique number.

These were in use up to the 60's and pop up regularly.

Here is a picture of an unused label I have which is in good condition. I suspect that it would have been identical to yours if we could of compared yours if in better condition. The black bit at the end ("Not a drop sold ...") is actually a separate label and placed where the red kidney label is on your bottle is and the red 3 stars label should be on the neck.
